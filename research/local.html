Janus A (JanA): Localization during conjugation and pharmacological reproduction of mutant phenotype in Tetrahymena thermophila

Fall 2023

Program

Independent Research - St. Olaf College, Northfield, Minnesota, USA

Abstract

This research project had two main goals - to see where the JanA gene product was localized during each stage of Tetrahymena thermophila conjugation, and to test the effects of 2 polo-like kinase (PLK) inhibitors (Volasertib and BI 2536) on wild-type Tetrahymena thermophila to find out if the janA loss-of-function phenotype could be pharmacologically reproduced. First, janA seemed to localize to micronuclei and mating junction through most of the conjugation process, then ended up in small punctae within the macronuclei during MAC anlagen development. This led to our hypothesis that janA would be involved in cell division and mitotic assembly similar to polo-like kinase. Second, Volasertib and BI 2536 caused dramatic broadening of the contractile vacuole pore domains (CVPs) in wild-type cells. In cells treated with BI 2536, we occasionally saw the formation of a second oral apparatus (OP) similar to the janus mutant phenotype. These results support the idea that janA in Tetrahymena was indeed homologous to PLKs, and we could use pharmacological methods to reproduce the janus phenotype. The observations also suggest that janA might co-localize with PDD1 - a chromodomain protein responsible for DNA elimination and foci formation in nuclei, as well as prediction that the effects of 2 PLK inhibitors might be greater if Tetrahymena thermophila had a janA enhancer gene.

Authors

Huy V. Huynh, Eric S. Cole

Fluorescence microscopy, indirect immunofluorescence (antibody) microscopy, drug concentration response, drug cytotoxicity, cell cultures, cell starvation, cell mating.
