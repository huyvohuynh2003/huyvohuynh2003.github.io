Mapping O-GlcNAc transferase (OGT) adaptor binding site along its TPR domain - Using a photoactivatable OGT library to locate adaptor protein binding sites

Fall 2023

Program

Independent Research - St. Olaf College, Northfield, Minnesota, USA

Abstract

O-linked beta-N-acetylglucosamine (O-GlcNAc) transferase (OGT) is the only human enzyme that transfers the O-GlcNAc sugar from UDP-GlcNAc to nuclear and cytoplasmic proteins. OGT glycosylates over 1,000 substrates involved in nearly all cellular processes, and misregulated protein O-GlcNAc levels have been implicated in cancer and neurodegenerative disease. How OGT selects its protein substrates is unknown, but it is proposed that OGT interacts with adaptor proteins that facilitate substrate selection through its tetratricopeptide (TPR) domain. We aim to locate adaptor binding sites along the TPR domain using a photoactivatable OGT mutant library. Here, we showed that upon UV irradiation of the OGT Bpa-incorporated mutants, we have covalently captured multiple potential binding sites along the solvent-exposed surface for the TPR domain for the known adaptor, CARM1.

Authors

Huy V. Huynh, Peter Leach, Meghan Moore, Tiarra Glogowski, Cassandra M. Joiner

Techniques

E. Coli cell cultures, protein expression induction, affinity chromatography, size-exclusion chromatography, SDS-PAGE, western blotting
