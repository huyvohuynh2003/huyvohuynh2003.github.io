Research Interest

During my undergraduate studies at St. Olaf, my research projects have varied in different concepts, including mutant cloning, protein crosslinking, testing medication on cellular patterns, and protein localization using fluorescence microscopy. I spent one summer at Mayo Clinic Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences modifying and testing different medications on enzymes by performing activity assays, as well as studying protein structure with crystallography screening. I also learned more about how misregulation of enzymatic levels could lead to abnormal developmental processes or physiological mishaps (such as cancer). Overtime, I definitely show more interest in protein biochemistry within the human body, as well as how certain molecules can alter these proteins’ folding and/or function. More specifically, studying how adjustment of enzymes can contribute to cellular signaling changes, and how clinical solutions can counteract those changes is definitely my top choice.
